About

HCA Survey

As the world continues to grapple with the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, one of the main challenges facing healthcare providers has been the shortage of medical resources and the lack of a clear plan for efficiently distributing them. This has made it difficult to provide adequate care for COVID-19 patients, particularly those who are at high risk for serious illness.

This website is dedicated to addressing this issue by providing a solution that utilizes Artificial Intelligence to analyze patient data, lifestyle information, and external sources to predict which individuals are likely to need extended hospital stays due to COVID-19. We are inviting you, the patient, to take a survey that will help us gather the information needed to make these predictions.

By taking this survey, you will be helping healthcare providers to better predict and prepare for the needs of their COVID-19 patients, ensuring that they receive the care they need in a timely manner.

The information you provide will be kept confidential and used solely for the purpose of improving healthcare for COVID-19 patients. We appreciate your participation and thank you for your support in this important effort.

Data Examination

Feature importance scores are a way to determine the relative importance of different features or variables in a dataset. These scores are typically calculated using algorithms such as decision trees, random forests, or gradient boosting, and they provide a way to understand which factors are most influential in the predictions made by a model. For example, in the case of predicting which patients are likely to need extended hospital stays due to COVID-19, feature importance scores can be used to determine which patient characteristics, such as age or underlying medical conditions, are most important in making these predictions.

In the data collected and used to train the current machine learning model, we identified age and diabetes as the two most influential factors in predicting extended hospitalization of a patient.

This visualization shows a U-Shaped Distribution that identifies the two extremes (Infant and Senior) as the most likely among all other ages to be hospitalized. This graph identifies, in detail, the importance of that feature to our model.
